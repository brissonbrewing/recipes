Beer 'n Brat Pasta

This is my wife's signature dish that she has made many times. It's fast and easy and awesome.

Beer 'n Brat Pasta

serves 2-4

Ingredients:
olive oil
one package (5 links) johnsonville bratwurst
one medium-bodied, low-hop beer (like Yuengling)
4 medium onions, diced
6 cloves garlic, minced
one can (14.5 ounce) petite diced tomatoes, or about a cup of freshly diced tomatoes
good parmesean cheese, shredded
One box pasta...bowties work well, but any bite-sized shape will do

In a large frying or chef pan, brown the brats on both sides with olive oil. Add the beer, cover, and turn temperature to low. Simmer for 4-5 minutes, turn brats, re-cover, and simmer for another 4-5 minutes. Remove the lid and raise the tenperature to medium, allowing the beer to cook down into a syrup. When the beer just starts to burn, add all of the onions. It may look like there are way too many onions, but I promise they will cook down.

Add salt to the onions, and toss/stir/flip them often. You may want to add more olive oil for this. Flip the brats once they've browned again, and remove them once both sides are re-browned. Let the brats cool on a cutting board.

Cook the onions until they are reduced by 1/3, stirring frequently. If they start to burn, you can add a few ounces of beer to deglaze the pan. When cooked down, add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Add the can of tomatoes (do not drain). Slice the brats into moons or half moons and re-add to the pan.

Cook for about 2 more minutes, then turn off. Add the pasta, and a quarter to half a cup of shredded parmesean cheese. Toss to coat.

Serve with a bit more cheese on top...and then die of yummy happiness.
